# General information
ICPC stands for International Collegiate Programming Contest.
It's a global algorithmic programming contest for college students.
It's the oldest, largest, and most prestigious programming contest in the world.
More than 50,000 students worldwide per year.
More than 3,000 universities in 111 countries.
Over 400 on-site competitions. Six different continents.
Only 1% advances to the world finals.

The ICPC features several levels of competition:
- Local Contests: 300,000 students compete in local contests and advance to regionals if selected.
  The local competitions happen among classmates to determine who may represent their university.
  8-12 problems will be posted.
- Regional Contests: 5 hour deadline with complex, real-world algorithmic problems.
  Team/team's that solve the greatest number of problems with the least number of attempts in the least cumulative time advance to world finals.
- Regional Championships
- The World Finals: Held each spring in a different location around the globe
